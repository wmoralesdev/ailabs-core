# Ai Labs Brand Guide

Working source of truth for how Ai Labs shows up in language, visuals, and materials.

Substance (what we are, pillars, who for) lives in `PRODUCT.md`. This file covers expression: name, voice, identity, type, color, texture, and co-branding.

## Brand Summary

Ai Labs helps companies adapt, develop, and learn with AI.

Three pillars:

- **Academy** — learn with us (consultancies, workshops, bootcamps, residencies).
- **Agentic** — build with us (software on demand, plus workflows and automation, done with AI).
- **Aperture** — connect with us (community and innovation hub: people, profiles, partner companies).

Co-founders: Walter Morales and Daniela Lopez — software engineers.

## Name Rules

### Primary written name

**Ai Labs**

Default for copy, SEO, social, decks, sales, and speech.

### Domain and handles

**ailabs.sv**

Use when pointing to the site, social, or local context.

### Stylized mark

**Ai /abs**

Only when the visual lockup or art direction makes the slash intentional.

Do not use the slash as the written name in normal sentences.

### Avoid

- Writing `Ai /abs` in body copy by default.
- Alternating casually between `Ai Labs`, `Ai /abs`, and `Ai Labs SV`.
- `AI Labs` unless an external platform forces that casing.

## Voice And Tone

Clear, direct, builder-to-builder. Technical when it helps. Light when it fits. Never forced.

### Do

- Say the thing simply.
- Be concise and complete.
- Ground claims in real work, events, teams, and proof.
- Sound like software engineers who also teach and convene.

### Do not

- Oversell or exaggerate.
- Force jokes or casual slang.
- Hide meaning in jargon or corporate speak.
- Make AI sound magical.
- Promise access, credits, or partner outcomes that are not confirmed.

### Tone swaps

Say: We help companies adapt, develop, and learn with AI.

Avoid: We empower organizations to unlock the transformative potential of AI.

Say: Bring Ai Labs to your team.

Avoid: Revolutionize your organization with next-generation AI enablement.

Say: Practical training, software work, and a real builder community.

Avoid: The future of AI innovation in Central America.

## Visual Direction

Inspiration: mem0’s calm structure and soft purple system — not a clone. Pair that with our own mark: a molecule-style **A** that reads as network, adapters, and connection.

Mood:

- Credible and practical.
- Soft where surfaces are light; sharp where the logo sits on graphite.
- Purple as signal, not wallpaper.
- Real people and real work over stock AI imagery.

Default modes:

1. **Light marketing** — white or soft lavender grounds, dark text, purple accent. Clear sections, generous space, simple navigation (mem0-like rhythm).
2. **Dark moments** — graphite grounds for logo contexts, event graphics, hero moments, and reverse lockups. Purple mark on dark; light type.

## Color

Observed from the current isotipo and working materials:

| Role              | Hex       | Use                                                      |
| ----------------- | --------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Brand purple      | `#A78BFA` | Mark, accents, CTAs, links, diagrams, pillar markers     |
| Graphite          | `#303030` | Dark grounds, dark UI, logo background, strong text      |
| Lavender wash     | `#EDE4FF` | Soft section backgrounds, footer fields (mem0-inspired)  |
| Stipple ink       | `#C4ACF6` | Dotted texture over lavender (sample from footer inspo)  |
| White             | `#FFFFFF` | Primary light surface, reverse type on dark              |
| Muted text        | `#5C5C5C` | Secondary copy on light                                  |

Notes:

- Brand purple `#A78BFA` is sampled from the current mark on graphite. Refine if source vectors use a different approved purple.
- Lavender wash and stipple ink are directionally inspired by mem0’s footer — adapt to our purple, do not paste their palette.
- Cream/off-white is optional; if used, pick one value and stick to it.
- Purple is a signal: CTAs, markers, diagrams, event graphics — not a full-page flood.

Recommended balance:

- Light pages: white + lavender washes + graphite text + purple accent.
- Dark moments: graphite + purple mark + white/light type.
- Never put the purple mark on a low-contrast wash without checking contrast.

## Logo

### Mark

Molecule / metaball **A** — organic connected blobs. Reads as nodes, network, and adaptation. Current file: purple mark on graphite.

### Lockup

Horizontal lockup can use the slash: `Ai /abs`. Slash is visual only.

### When to use what

- **Mark alone** — favicons, avatars, badges, pattern moments, slides where the wordmark already appears.
- **Wordmark / lockup** — headers, footers, decks, anything that needs the name readable.
- **On graphite** — preferred for standalone brand moments and event frames.
- **On white / lavender** — fine for site chrome when contrast stays clear; prefer graphite or single-color mark as needed.

### Do not

- Redraw or stretch the mark.
- Recolor outside approved brand colors.
- Place purple-on-light or purple-on-dark without contrast checks.
- Make the slash the SEO or spoken naming default.

## Typography

Open fonts (Google Fonts):

| Role    | Font                   | Use                                      |
| ------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Titles  | **Bricolage Grotesque** | H1–H3, display, section titles, hero     |
| Body/UI | **DM Sans**             | Body, nav, buttons, forms, captions      |

Why this pairing:

- Bricolage carries personality in titles without looking like generic AI SaaS.
- DM Sans stays open, quiet, and readable for long copy and UI — less default than Inter.

Rules:

- Titles in Bricolage; do not set long paragraphs in it.
- Body and UI in DM Sans.
- Hierarchy through size, weight, and spacing — not through more fonts.
- Avoid decorative or “futuristic” display faces.

## Texture And Pattern

### Organic stipple (halftone-style fill)

What you pointed at in the mem0 footer is best described as an **organic stipple** (also: dithered or halftone-style fill): many small dots forming a soft, branching network shape that sits inside a lavender wash and fades at the edges.

Use for Ai Labs:

- Section washes and footers.
- Soft brand fields behind content — not a full-page wallpaper.
- Built from our lavender + stipple ink, optional echoes of the molecule mark.

Do not:

- Steal mem0 artwork.
- Make the stipple louder than the content.
- Use it on every surface.

Optional secondary pattern: quiet echoes of the molecule **A** at very low opacity for event graphics and slide decks — sparingly.

## Layout Rhythm (Site)

Borrow structure habits from mem0, not their product language:

- Calm top nav, clear primary CTA.
- Hero that states what Ai Labs is in one breath.
- Proof strip with real metrics and partner context.
- Three pillar blocks with plain descriptors.
- Soft lavender + stipple sections for community/partner/footer.
- Dark band or lockup moment where the brand mark can sit on graphite.

Keep whitespace. Prefer columns and lists over dense marketing walls.

## Imagery

Prefer:

- Workshops, hackathons, demos, builders at work.
- Real screens from products and prototypes.
- Partner and ambassador moments when accurate.
- Event photos and recaps.

Avoid:

- Glowing robots and neon brain stock.
- Generic “AI gradient” mash.
- Fake server rooms.
- People pointing at empty glass boards.

## Pillar Visual Cues

Pillars share the same system. Emphasis shifts; colors and fonts do not fork into sub-brands.

### Academy

People learning, sessions, exercises, cohorts. Tone: practical and clear.

### Agentic

Products, workflows, diagrams, tools, screens. Tone: precise and outcome-focused.

### Aperture

Events, introductions, density of community, partner moments. Tone: connected and specific — never “access” without saying to what.

## Co-Branding

Rules:

- Name the exact relationship (ambassador, Regional Lead, partner, sponsor).
- Do not imply official partnership unless confirmed.
- Do not promise third-party credits or access unless approved.
- Sponsor value is practical: audience, builders, learning, demos, feedback, participation.
- Logos are support, not the product.

Use: Supported by partners helping builders learn and build with real tools.

Avoid: Powered by the world’s leading AI ecosystem.

Use: Ai Labs can help design the program, reach the right builders, and facilitate engagement.

Avoid: Ai Labs guarantees access to top AI companies and credits.
